# Special Interest Groups (SIGs)

## Overview
Special Interest Groups (SIGs) are focused teams within the Confidential Computing community that address specific technical domains and collaborative efforts.

## Current SIGs

### Kernel SIG
- **Focus**: Linux kernel development and confidential computing integration
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Kernel-level confidential computing support
  - Open-source kernel technologies
  - Security and performance optimizations

#### Quick Links
- [Kernel SIG Repository](https://github.com/confidential-computing/governance/tree/main/SIGs/Kernel)
- Contact: kernel-sig@confidentialcomputing.io

### Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) SIG
- **Focus**: Regulatory frameworks, compliance standards, and governance models
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Developing compliance guidelines
  - Risk assessment methodologies
  - Standardization efforts

#### Quick Links
- [GRC SIG Repository](https://github.com/confidential-computing/governance/tree/main/SIGs/GRC)
- Contact: grc-sig@confidentialcomputing.io

## Getting Involved
1. Review the SIG charters
2. Join our communication channels
3. Participate in meetings and discussions
4. Contribute to ongoing projects

## Contribution Guidelines
- Follow our [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
- Submit pull requests with clear descriptions
- Attend monthly SIG meetings

## Meeting Schedule
- Kernel SIG: First Wednesday of each month
- GRC SIG: Third Thursday of each month

## Communication Channels
- Slack: #sig-kernel, #sig-grc
- Mailing Lists: kernel-sig@lists.confidentialcomputing.io, grc-sig@lists.confidentialcomputing.io
